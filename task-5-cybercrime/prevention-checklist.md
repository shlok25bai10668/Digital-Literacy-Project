Stay Safe Online — Checklist

(Relevant for students in India | Report cybercrime at cybercrime.gov.in or call Helpline 1930)

Account & Password Security

- Use strong, unique passwords for every account — combine uppercase, lowercase, numbers, and symbols. Never reuse the same password across platforms like Instagram, Gmail, and your college portal.
- Enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on all critical accounts — email, social media, and banking apps. Even if your password is stolen, 2FA blocks unauthorized access.
- Never share OTPs with anyone — no legitimate bank, company, or government agency will ever call and ask for your One-Time Password. Hang up immediately if asked.

Financial & UPI Safety

- Set a daily UPI transaction limit in apps like PhonePe, GPay, or Paytm. Keep it low (₹2,000–5,000) for day-to-day use to minimize loss in case of compromise.
- Never scan a QR code to receive money — receiving payments never requires scanning. Scammers send fake "receive money" QR codes that actually deduct funds from your account.
- Avoid using UPI or net banking on public Wi-Fi — coffee shops, college canteen hotspots, and public networks are easy interception points for attackers.

Phishing & Scam Awareness

- Verify links before clicking, hover over URLs in emails or messages to check the actual destination. Fake sites often use domains like "sbi-secure-login.com" instead of "sbi.co.in."
- Be skeptical of "You've won a prize!" or "Urgent KYC update" messages, these are among the most common phishing lures targeting Indian college students via WhatsApp and SMS.

Device & App Safety

- Only download apps from official stores (Google Play Store / Apple App Store). Avoid APK files shared via WhatsApp or Telegram, as they frequently contain malware.
- Keep your phone's OS and apps updated — updates patch security vulnerabilities that attackers actively exploit.

Social Media & Privacy

- Audit your social media privacy settings every semester — check who can see your posts, phone number, and location on Instagram, Snapchat, and LinkedIn.
- Do not accept friend/follow requests from unknown accounts, especially those with few posts, no profile picture, or suspiciously new accounts — common traits of fake profiles used for social engineering.

Report Immediately

Channel - Details
National Cyber Crime Portal - [cybercrime.gov.in](https://cybercrime.gov.in) — File online complaints for fraud, identity theft, and harassment 
Helpline 1930 - Call anytime for immediate assistance with financial cyber fraud — act within the first few hours to freeze stolen funds 

> ⚡ **Pro tip:** In UPI fraud cases, time is critical. Calling **1930 within the first 30–60 minutes** significantly increases the chance of freezing and recovering stolen money before it is transferred further.
