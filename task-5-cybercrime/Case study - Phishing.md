Phishing is a type of cyberattack where criminals impersonate trusted entities such as banks, government agencies, or popular services to trick individuals into revealing sensitive information like passwords, credit card numbers, or personal data.

How It Happens:

First, the attacker collects target email addresses and selects a trusted brand or organization to impersonate. Then, a fake website or spoofed email address is created that closely resembles the legitimate source. Next, a mass email is sent with urgent language, pressuring the victim to act quickly without thinking. The victim clicks the link, lands on the fake site, enters their credentials, and the attacker captures everything.

Who Is Targeted?
Anyone with an online presence can be targeted, but cybercriminals particularly pursue individuals with access to financial data, healthcare records, or corporate systems. Over 90% of targeted cyberattacks begin with a phishing email. More sophisticated "spear phishing" attacks zero in on specific employees, executives, and even college students who are less security-aware.

Consequences
The fallout from a successful phishing attack can be severe:

Financial loss.

Identity theft.

Data breaches.

Reputational damage.

Ransomware infections.
